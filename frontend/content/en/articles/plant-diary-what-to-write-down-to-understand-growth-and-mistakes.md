A plant diary is needed not for beautiful notes, but for checking the reasons. Without records, it seems that the problem appeared suddenly: the leaves drooped, growth stopped, the substrate began to dry out strangely. The diary shows that a week before they changed the light, increased watering, moved the pot or added fertilizing. It is this connection that helps correct care without random experiments.

A good diary doesn't have to be long. It is better to regularly record several important events than to write a detailed report once a month. In GrowerHub it is convenient to record as a time line: action, parameters, photo and comment. Then watering, sensor graphics and the appearance of the plant are in the same context.

## What to write down every time

Minimal entry: date, plant or zone, action and reason. For example: “watering 400 ml, the top layer is dry, the sensor is below the normal range” or “moved closer to the window, there is not enough light according to the photo.” This phrase is more useful than just “watered.” She explains why the action was done.

For watering, it is worth recording the volume, type of water, pH if necessary, the presence of fertilizers and the reaction of the plant after a day or two. For replanting - the new volume of the pot, the composition of the substrate and the condition of the roots, if you have seen them. For treatment - drug, concentration, plant area and reason. Irrigation records are discussed in detail in the article [irrigation log: volume, pH, fertilizers](/articles/zhurnal-poliva-obem-ph-udobreniya).

## Photos are more important than they seem

Memory does not store slow changes well. The plant becomes longer, the leaves become lighter or the crown gradually becomes thinner. A photo once a week under the same conditions shows the dynamics better than the impression “it seems to be worse.” Shoot from one side, under similar lighting and at approximately the same distance. If the light or location changes, make a note of it in the recording.

Photos are useful to associate with data. For example, if the pictures show wilting and the moisture graph shows frequent watering, there may be overwatering or a root problem. If the photo shows stretching, look at the light and temperature. There are separate rules in the article [photo diary of plant growth](/articles/foto-dnevnik-rosta-rasteniy).

## What data should not be recorded

Don't turn your diary into an accounting of everything. If a parameter does not help make a decision, it can be omitted. For a house plant, watering, photos, replanting, fertilizing and noticeable symptoms are often more important. For the greenhouse, a microclimate, a zone, a person in charge and equipment maintenance are added. A diary that is too detailed is quickly abandoned, and an empty diary does not help the analysis.

A useful filter is simple: in a month, using this entry, will you be able to understand what you changed and why. If yes, the recording is good. If it's just a number without context, its value is lower.

## How a diary helps an advisor

The care advisor should not draw conclusions from one symptom. Yellow leaves can mean different problems, and without a history, it's easy to make a mistake. But if there is a humidity chart nearby, the last waterings, pH, a photo and the date of transplantation, the recommendation becomes more careful and accurate. At GrowerHub, this is exactly what a diary is for: to give context to data, not to replace observation.

The graphs themselves also require some explanation. A sharp jump in temperature could be an open greenhouse door, or it could be a ventilation failure. If the event is noted in the diary, the interpretation does not turn into fortune telling. More details about graphs can be found in the article [history of microclimate and graphs](/articles/istoriya-mikroklimata-i-grafiki).

## Conclusion

Write down not everything, but the important things: watering, fertilizing, transplanting, treatments, photos, symptoms and changes in conditions. Add the reason for the action and the result after a few days. Such a diary helps you see patterns, build a weekly care plan, and trust automation more easily.

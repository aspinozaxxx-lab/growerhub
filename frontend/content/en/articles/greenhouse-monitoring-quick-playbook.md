Greenhouse monitoring should help you quickly spot a problem and understand who is solving it. You don't need complex analytics to get started. You need zones, several reliable sensors, notification priorities, and a history habit. This playbook can be used as a short run order for GrowerHub in a small greenhouse.

## Step 1: separate zones

Describe the zones as a person sees them on site: the left bed, the rack of seedlings, the box with herbs, the area by the door. For each zone, specify plants, sensors, responsible person and main risks. If all devices are on a common list, monitoring will quickly become inconvenient.

For multiple boxes, use the same card structure. This is discussed in more detail in the article [monitoring multiple boxes](/articles/monitoring-neskolkih-boksov).

## Step 2: Connect a minimum of sensors

Start with air temperature and humidity, soil moisture at the control point, leakage and watering conditions. If there is light and ventilation, add their status. Do not install sensors without deciding what to do if there is a deviation. Each parameter must have meaning.

A checklist of basic sensors is in the article [checklist of sensors for a greenhouse](/articles/sensor-checklist).

## Step 3: set up alarms

Divide notifications into accidents, risks and maintenance. Leakage and overheating are an accident. Rapid drop in humidity or low battery - risk or maintenance. If all messages are the same, important things will be lost.

The notification must include the zone, reason, and system action. For example: “Seedling rack: temperature above threshold 40 minutes, ventilation on.” Details are in the article [notifications in the mini-farm](/articles/uvedomleniya-v-mini-ferme).

## Step 4: Daily inspection

Every day check accidents, freshness of sensors, water reservoir, trays, plant condition and recent watering events. If everything is fine, do not change the settings. Monitoring is needed not for constant edits, but for calm control.

## Step 5: weekly analysis

Once a week, look at the graphs: temperature peaks, nighttime humidity, water flow, watering frequency, sensor omissions and manual actions. Write down one or two resolutions for the next week. If you change many parameters at once, the result will be difficult to explain.

## Conclusion

Fast greenhouse monitoring is based on a simple procedure: zones, minimum sensors, notification priorities, daily inspection and weekly analysis. GrowerHub helps to maintain this structure and not drown in individual numbers.

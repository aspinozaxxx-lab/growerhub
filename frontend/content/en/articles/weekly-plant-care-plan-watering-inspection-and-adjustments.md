A weekly care plan helps avoid confusing regularity with ongoing intervention. Plants need stability, and the owner needs a clear rhythm: when to check humidity, when to take photos, when to look at graphs, when to maintain equipment. Without a plan, it’s easy to change settings every day and then not understand what exactly influenced growth.

The plan should not be a rigid schedule for every situation. It sets up basic checks, and GrowerHub helps you see exceptions: drying out, overheating, missing a sensor, leaking, or unusual graph behavior. This way the care remains calm, but not blind.

## Daily quick checks

Every day, a short inspection is enough: the appearance of the leaves, the condition of the trays, the presence of water in the tank, the freshness of sensor data and emergency notifications. If everything is fine, there is no need to change the settings. Too frequent adjustments make it difficult to see the effect of the previous action.

For automatic watering, see the startup log. Were there waterings more often than usual, was the limit triggered, was there a manual switch on? If automation suddenly becomes more active, this is a reason to check the sensor, weather, ventilation and drips.

## One day for analysis

Set aside time once a week to quietly review your data. Open the graphs of humidity, temperature and light, compare them with your diary entries. What has changed? Was it a hot day, new fertilizing, replanting, changing a lamp, cleaning the ventilation? It is better to see such connections at once, and not to catch them by one alarm.

It is convenient to plan adjustments on the same day. If you need to change the humidity threshold or light schedule, make one change and write down the reason. In a week it will be clear whether it helped or not. The principles of record keeping are described in the article [plant diary](/articles/dnevnik-rasteniya-chto-zapisyvat).

## Photo and growth stage

Take photos of the plant on the same day of the week. The photo shows slow changes: stretching, compaction of the crown, change in color, damage to the leaves. In GrowerHub it is useful to associate photos with watering, fertilizing and the growth stage. Then the report does not turn into a set of pictures.

Growth stage affects care. Seedlings, active growth, flowering in ornamental crops, fruiting in vegetables or recovery after transplanting require different expectations. You don't need to change all the parameters every time, but the weekly plan should take into account the stage. Read more about this in the article [stages of growth and care settings](/articles/stadii-rosta-i-nastroyki-uhoda).

## Equipment maintenance

Check your tubing, IVs, reservoir, sensors, and power supply once a week. Automatic watering can work perfectly in a magazine, but one pinched tube changes the result. For sensors, the condition of the cable, fastening and freshness of data are important. For the pump - sound, flow and no leaks.

If the system is maintained by several people, record who did what. This is especially important for a greenhouse or mini-farm: otherwise one person changes the setting, the second sees a different result and does not understand the reason.

## Conclusion

A weekly care plan is a way to maintain a balance between observation and intervention. Check safety and obvious symptoms every day, analyze graphs, photos and journal once a week, and make changes one at a time. This rhythm makes GrowerHub's recommendations clearer and reduces the number of random decisions.

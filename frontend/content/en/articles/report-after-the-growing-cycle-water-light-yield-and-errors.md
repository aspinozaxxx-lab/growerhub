The report after the cycle is needed not for beautiful statistics, but for the next decision. What worked, where was the excess water consumption, when problems appeared, which settings should be repeated and which should be changed. Without a report, a new cycle starts almost from scratch, even if GrowerHub has accumulated graphs and records.

The cycle can be understood not only as the harvest in the greenhouse. For a houseplant, this may be the period after transplantation, a season of active growth, or several months of observation. The main thing is to choose a beginning and an end so that the comparison is fair.

## What to include in the report

Minimal report: start and end date, plants or zone, important stages, total water consumption, number of waterings, major changes in light and microclimate, fertilizing, before and after photos, problems and solutions. For productive crops, the mass or quantity of the result is added; for ornamental crops, the quality of growth, flowering, and crown condition are added.

Don't try to explain everything with one parameter. A good report shows the chain. For example: in the middle of the cycle it became hotter, ventilation worked longer, soil moisture fell faster, watering became more frequent, and water consumption increased. This conclusion is more useful than “you need to water more.”

## Water and irrigation

See not only the total consumption, but also the distribution over time. Have there been any weeks with a sharp increase in consumption? Did they coincide with heat, stage of growth, or sensor error? Did the limits work? Were there hand waterings that were not included in the automatic rule?

For a greenhouse, the water report is related to resources and maintenance. If the consumption has increased, you need to understand whether it is normal plant growth or a leak, clogging, or change in weather. A separate material [accounting for water and irrigation in a greenhouse](/articles/uchet-vody-i-polivov-v-teplitse) reveals this side in more detail.

## Photo and microclimate

Photos help you see when the plant has actually changed. Place pictures of the beginning, middle and end of the cycle side by side. Then look at graphs of temperature, humidity, and light around problem periods. If elongation appears in the photo, look for a connection with the light. If there were spots or lethargy, look at watering, air humidity and diary events.

The photo diary should be part of the report, not a separate folder. How to take comparable pictures is described in the article [growth photo diary](/articles/foto-dnevnik-rosta-rasteniy).

## Errors and solutions

The most valuable part of the report is not the list of successes, but the list of solutions for the next cycle. For example: “divide the zone into two lines”, “move the sensor further from the dripper”, “reduce evening watering”, “add a leakage sensor”, “check the filter every week”. Such insights turn data into process improvements.

For a mini-farm, the final report can be linked to crops and resources. This is discussed in more detail in the article [harvest and resource reports](/articles/otchety-po-urozhayu-i-resursam).

## Conclusion

The final report should answer the question: what will we repeat, what will we change and why. Water, light, microclimate, photo, magazine and result together give a clear picture. Then the next cycle of GrowerHub begins not with guesses, but with accumulated experience.

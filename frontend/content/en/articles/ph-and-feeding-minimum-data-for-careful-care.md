pH and fertilizing can easily become unnecessary complexity. The owner begins to measure everything, change dosages every week and loses touch with the real state of the plant. The correct minimum of data helps in another way: to record the composition of irrigation, not to forget changes and not to draw sharp conclusions based on one symptom.

For many unpretentious indoor plants, stable water and careful watering are sufficient. pH becomes more important when you use nutrient solutions, sensitive crops, hydroponic elements, seedlings, or notice recurring problems after fertilizing. In any case, the data must be verifiable.

## What to write down

Minimum entry for fertilizing: date, plant or zone, volume of solution, name of fertilizer, dosage, pH of prepared solution if necessary and reason for application. The reason is important: planned feeding, recovery after transplantation, visible deficiency, active growth stage. Without a reason, after a month it’s difficult to understand why you changed your diet.

If you use a pH meter, write down what exactly you measured: the source water or the finished solution. Calibrate the device periodically and do not treat the last number as an absolute truth. An inaccurate device plus frequent adjustments is worse than a stable simple mode.

## Why you can't change everything at once

One of the common mistakes is to simultaneously increase watering, add fertilizer, change the light and rearrange the plant. If it gets better or worse after a week, the reason is unknown. Therefore, it is useful to record changes separately in the GrowerHub journal and give the plant time to respond.

If a nutritional problem is suspected, first check the base: moisture, drainage, root condition, light, temperature and recent activity. Symptoms of deficiency and overflow may overlap. The material [overfilling or underfilling according to sensor data](/articles/pereliv-ili-nedoliv-po-dannym-datchika) helps not to attribute every problem to fertilizers.

## How to connect with watering

Fertilizing always occurs through water or near irrigation. Therefore, the food log should be part of the watering log and not a separate note. If the plant received the solution, it is important to see the total volume of water for the week. Otherwise, you can accidentally increase the power along with the overflow.

The article [irrigation log: volume, pH, fertilizers](/articles/zhurnal-poliva-obem-ph-udobreniya) describes a convenient set of fields. For GrowerHub the task is simple: to show when it was ordinary water, when it was a solution, what the volume was and whether there were symptoms after that.

## When an advisor should be careful

The adviser should not recommend fertilizer based on one photo. This recommendation requires watering history, growth stage, recent fertilizing, pH, microclimate and confirmation that the problem is not caused by overwatering or drying out. If there is little data, the correct recommendation is to check the conditions and record the observation, rather than immediately change the composition.

## Conclusion

pH and feeding are useful when they are built into a calm journal. Record the composition of the solution, volume, cause and reaction of the plant. Don't change multiple factors at once. So GrowerHub helps you see the connection between nutrition and growth, rather than multiplying random adjustments.
